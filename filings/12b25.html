General Information & Instructions

Quick Summary

  • What: Form 12B-25 is an notification of late filing.
  • Why: It is required by SEC Mandate to ensure fair disclosure, transparency, and regulatory compliance.
  • When: This is generally an event-based filing. It must be filed within 4 business days.
  • Who: It must be filed by public entities.

Expert Insight (DFIN)

Most companies that sell securities, such as a publicly owned company with stocks available for purchase, need to file reports on forms 10-D, 10-Q, 10-K, 11-K, 20-F, N-CEN, and N-CSR with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission. These reports give a detailed analysis of the company’s revenue generation, overall expenses and other relevant data.

What Is SEC Form 12b-25?

Businesses may need to file regular reports about their financial concerns with the SEC. These filings provide crucial transparency to a company’s investors, as well as regulators. When businesses cannot file a quarterly or annual report by the deadline date for reasons, such as a failure of a system that generates those financial reports, they may need to file SEC Form 12b-25. This form relates to late filing of reports, such as Form 10-D, Form 10-K, Form 10-Q, Form 11-K, Form 20-F, N-CEN, and N-CSR. The form alerts the SEC that the company is late in filing the report while they look for ways to solve the problem. It’s important to note that filing this form does not excuse the filing of the original report, only delays it.

Key Components of SEC Form 12b-25

Simply, SEC Form 12b-25 notifies the SEC that an organization will be late in filing a particular report. To avoid penalties or other consequences, companies must file this form within one business day after the original deadline. By filing this request for an extension, businesses can get 5 to 15 calendar days added to the deadline for the report, depending on the type. Note that these are not business days, so weekends count toward the filing deadline.

Likewise, causes that fall into a pattern of recurring delays may be flagged by SEC as unacceptable reasons for filing.

Implications of Filing SEC Form 12b-25

Filing Form 12b-25 has a number of implications for the company and its investors besides general compliance with SEC guidelines. Although the form provides a short extension, businesses must still file within the deadline to avoid penalties for late filing. Failing to meet the deadline can lead to additional consequences. Filing Form 12b-25 does not guarantee that the company will avoid penalties.

Additionally, these reports are published publicly and can affect the company’s standing in the market. Investors use reports to help guide their investment decisions. A late filing, particularly if the company needs an extension on a regular basis, may indicate that the business is struggling. Providing details about the delay may put investors at ease or give them reason to plan an exit strategy.
