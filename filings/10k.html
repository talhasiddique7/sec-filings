General Information & Instructions

Filing Summary

  • What: Comprehensive annual report: audited financials (income statement, balance sheet, cash flows), MD&A, business description, risk factors, properties, legal proceedings, executive compensation, and internal controls assessment.
  • Why: Gold standard of financial disclosure. Every revenue, EPS, margin, and debt metric on Yahoo Finance / Finviz / Macrotrends flows from 10-K data. Without it you cannot build a proper DCF, compare historical financials, or verify audited results.
  • When: 60 days (large accelerated), 75 days (accelerated), or 90 days (non-accelerated) after fiscal year-end.
  • Who: All domestic public companies registered under Exchange Act Sections 12 or 15(d).

Professional Insight

The 10-K is the 'gold standard' for fundamental analysis. It's used to build discounted cash flow (DCF) models, assess long-term competitive moats, and verify the integrity of reported earnings via the auditor's report. Professional analysts skip the investor presentation and go straight to the 10-K to find hidden liabilities, pension obligations, and lease commitments that aren't visible in summary data.

Understanding the 10-K Filing

The definitive annual report for US public companies. It contains audited financial statements including the Balance Sheet, Income Statement, and Cash Flow Statement. Crucially, it includes the MD&A (Item 7), where management explains the 'why' behind the numbers, and Item 1A (Risk Factors), detailing everything that could go wrong. It also covers legal proceedings, executive compensation, and the effectiveness of internal controls (SOX 404).

Filing Requirements and Compliance

Ensuring compliance with SEC regulations requires timely and accurate submission of the 10-K. The mandated timeframe for this form is 60 days (large accelerated), 75 days (accelerated), or 90 days (non-accelerated) after fiscal year-end. . Filing entities—specifically All domestic public companies registered under Exchange Act Sections 12 or 15(d). —must adhere rigidly to these deadlines to avoid regulatory penalties, maintain good standing on public exchanges, and preserve investor trust.

Submitting the document typically involves formatting the data via EDGAR (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval system), often utilizing standardized XBRL tagging to allow for immediate programmatic analysis by market participants. Delays or material omissions in these disclosures can result in severe repercussions, making a streamlined reporting process essential for modern corporate compliance.

Part II Reference

Core Financial Statements

Based on SEC mandates, this form requires the following audited or unaudited financial comparisons:

Balance Sheet

A statement of the assets, liabilities, and capital of a business at a particular point in time, detailing the balance of income and expenditure over the preceding period.

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.

Income Statement

A financial statement that reports a company's financial performance over a specific accounting period. Also known as Profit and Loss (P&L).

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.

Cash Flow Statement

A statement that provides aggregate data regarding all cash inflows a company receives from its ongoing operations and external investment sources.

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.

PART I

Section Overview

Item 1

Business

A comprehensive narrative of the company's core operations, subsidiaries, and competitive strategy. Focus on revenue drivers, seasonal trends, and intellectual property portfolios that define the company's market position.

Item 1A

Risk Factors

Critically important section detailing everything from macroeconomic headwinds and regulatory changes to specific operational vulnerabilities. Analysts look here for 'black swan' risks and management's risk mitigation awareness.

Item 1C

Cybersecurity

New SEC Mandate. Disclosures regarding the registrant's processes for the assessment, identification, and management of material risks from cybersecurity threats, and the board's oversight of these risks.

Item 2

Properties

Inventory of major physical assets, including headquarters, manufacturing facilities, and real estate. Important for asset-heavy industries to assess capacity and geographical footprint.

Item 3

Legal Proceedings

Disclosure of significant pending or threatened litigation that could materially impact financial standing. Footnotes here often reveal the true scope of potential liabilities.

PART II

Financial Performance

Item 7

MD&A

Management's Discussion & Analysis. This is the 'soul' of the report where management explains fluctuations in liquidity, capital resources, and results of operations. It connects the dots between the numbers.

Item 7A

Market Risk

Quantitative and qualitative sensitivity analysis regarding interest rate volatility, currency exchange shifts, and commodity price changes. Essential for modeling risk exposure.

Item 8

Financial Statements

The audited 'Ground Truth.' Contains the Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Statement of Cash Flows, and the Auditor's Report. This is where precision and regulatory compliance are paramount.

Item 9A

Controls & Procedures

Management's assessment of internal control over financial reporting (ICFR). A 'material weakness' disclosure here is a massive red flag for accounting integrity.

PART III

Governance & Pay

Item 10

Directors & Governance

Details on the Board of Directors, their background, and committee structures. Used to evaluate board independence and corporate governance health.

Item 11

Executive Pay

Full disclosure of compensation for Named Executive Officers (NEOs). Shows the alignment between executive incentives and shareholder value creation.

Item 12

Security Ownership

Table showing how much stock is held by insiders and 5% beneficial owners. High insider ownership often signals long-term alignment with shareholders.

Exhibits & Summary

Exhibits & Schedules
