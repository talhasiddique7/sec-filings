General Information & Instructions

Quick Summary

  • What: Registers American Depositary Receipts (ADRs) representing securities of foreign private issuers. Each ADR corresponds to a specific number of underlying foreign shares.
  • Why: It is required by SEC Mandate to ensure fair disclosure, transparency, and regulatory compliance.
  • When: Filed before ADR program commences.
  • Who: Depositary banks (e.g., JPMorgan, Citibank) establishing ADR programs.

What is SEC Form F-6?

The SEC F-6 form is an essential form for companies that wish to offer what are known as American Depositary Receipts or ADRs from a foreign company. Depositary banks issue ADRs representing foreign shares. Form F-6 registers these American depositary shares, but not the underlying foreign securities in a direct fashion. There are three levels of ADR programs, as detailed below:

No full SEC registration (Form F-6 only)

Minimal – home country reporting; limited U.S. disclosure

Entry-level U.S. market presence without raising capital

U.S. national exchanges (NYSE, NASDAQ)

Full SEC reporting as a public foreign issuer

Increase visibility and liquidity in U.S. markets

Full SEC reporting plus offering disclosures

Who Needs to File Form F-6?

The F-6 may seem like a straightforward form for recordkeeping purposes, but there is another reason it is required.

The information required within the form helps consumers better understand the security on offer, so they can decide if it matches their investment goals. The information also helps to deter securities fraud, by imposing transparency standards on foreign-issued offerings.

Two common mistakes to watch out for when preparing the F-6 include not calculating the fees properly and missing or inaccurate information, such as a missing English translation of the company name. In other cases, there may be issues stemming from improper formatting for EDGAR. For this reason, it is important to verify the form before submitting it.
