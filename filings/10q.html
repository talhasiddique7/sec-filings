General Information & Instructions

Quick Summary

  • What: Form 10-Q is an general.
  • Why: It is required by SEC Mandate to ensure fair disclosure, transparency, and regulatory compliance.
  • When: This is generally an quarterly filing. It must be filed within 45 days.
  • Who: It must be filed by public entities.

Rule as to Use of Form 1O-Q. 1. Form 10-Q shall be used for quarterly reports under Section 13 or 15(d) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (15 U.S.C. 78m or 78o(d)), filed pursuant to Rule 13a-13 (17 CFR 240.13a-13) or Rule 15d-13 (17 CFR 240.15d-13). A quarterly report on this Form pursuant to Rule 13a-13 or Rule 15d-13 shall be filed within the following period after the end of each of the first three fiscal quarters of each fiscal year, but no report need be filed for the fourth quarter of any fiscal year: a. 40 days after the end of the fiscal quarter for large accelerated filers and accelerated filers (as defined in 17 CFR § 240.12b-2); and b. 45 days after the end of the fiscal quarter for all other registrants. Part II Reference

Core Financial Statements

Based on SEC mandates, this form requires the following audited or unaudited financial comparisons:

Balance Sheet

A statement of the assets, liabilities, and capital of a business at a particular point in time, detailing the balance of income and expenditure over the preceding period.

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.

Income Statement

A financial statement that reports a company's financial performance over a specific accounting period. Also known as Profit and Loss (P&L).

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.

Cash Flow Statement

A statement that provides aggregate data regarding all cash inflows a company receives from its ongoing operations and external investment sources.

Required disclosure per Item 8 or Regulation S-X.
