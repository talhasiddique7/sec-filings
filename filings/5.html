General Information & Instructions

Filing Summary

  • What: Year-end filing for insiders to report transactions that were either exempt from Form 4 or not timely reported. Acts as an annual reconciliation of all insider activity.
  • Why: Catches unreported small transactions and gifts that affect total insider ownership counts. Important for ownership accuracy in ESG and governance scoring systems.
  • When: Within 45 days after the company's fiscal year-end.
  • Who: Insiders with deferred or exempt transactions during the fiscal year.

Professional Insight

A cleanup document. While less market-moving than Form 4, it ensures institutional databases have the correct final share counts for governance and valuation purposes.

Understanding the 5 Filing

Annual reconciliation of insider ownership. Reports transactions that were either exempt from Form 4 or failed to be reported on time during the year. Acts as the final cumulative check on insider data.

By mandating this disclosure, the SEC ensures that financial markets operate with maximum transparency. For stakeholders, analyzing this form removes ambiguity and establishes a reliable factual basis for making informed decisions.

Delays or material omissions in these disclosures can result in severe repercussions, making a streamlined reporting process essential for modern corporate compliance.
