General Information

General Information & Instructions

Filing Summary

  • What: Annual disclosure of whether a company's manufactured products contain conflict minerals (gold, tin, tungsten, tantalum) from the Democratic Republic of Congo or adjoining countries. Requires supply chain due diligence and independent audit if conflict minerals are present.
  • Why: ESG supply chain transparency. Impact investors and ESG raters use SD filings to score companies on conflict minerals sourcing practices. Tech companies (consumer electronics, semiconductors) file extensively.
  • When: Annually by May 31 for prior calendar year.
  • Who: SEC-reporting manufacturers whose products use conflict minerals necessary to production or functionality.

Professional Insight

The primary data source for ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) scores regarding supply chain ethics. Institutional investors use SD filings to ensure the companies they invest in aren't indirectly financing regional conflicts through their mineral procurement.

Understanding the SD Filing

The 'Specialized Disclosure' report, most commonly used for Conflict Minerals disclosures. Companies must disclose if their products contain gold, tin, tungsten, or tantalum sourced from the Democratic Republic of the Congo or adjoining countries.

Key Form Components

Deep-dive details regarding the specific regulatory requirements and analytical focuses for this filing:

Trace

Origin Inquiry

Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry (RCOI) details where the company attempted to trace conflict minerals to their source mines.

ESG

Due Diligence

Describes the supply chain framework used (usually OECD) to ensure minerals don't finance African warlords.

Audit

Independent Rev.

Confirms if an independent private sector audit was required and links to the full Conflict Minerals Report.
