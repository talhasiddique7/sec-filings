Form Overview & Purpose

Quick Summary

  • What: Form X-17F-1A is used to report securities that have been stolen, lost, or found to be counterfeit.
  • Why: To alert the SEC, the FBI, and the "SEC Lost and Stolen Securities Program" (LSSP) to prevent the illegal negotiation of these certificates.
  • When: Filing is required immediately upon discovery of theft and within strict windows for other losses.
  • Who: Filed by "Reporting Institutions" including broker-dealers, banks, and clearing agencies.

Expert Insight

Form X-17F-1A acts as a critical safety net for the secondary market. By centralizing the data of lost and stolen certificates, the SEC's Lost and Stolen Securities Program (LSSP) allows financial institutions to "check" certificates before they accept them for transfer, effectively neutralizing the value of stolen paper securities.

The Role of Reporting Institutions

Under Section 17(f)(1) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, almost all financial intermediaries are designated as "Reporting Institutions." This includes all members of the National Securities Exchange, registered broker-dealers, and most banking institutions. These entities are legally mandated to report any instance where they discover that a security in their custody or control has gone missing or is potentially counterfeit.

Reporting Requirements

Reporting follows a tiered system based on the nature of the security incident. Institutions must use Form X-17F-1A to notify the Securities Information Center (SIC), which manages the program on the SEC's behalf.

What must be reported?

  • ✅ Stolen Securities: Any security evidence of theft.
  • ✅ Lost or Missing Securities: Where custody cannot be accounted for.
  • ✅ Counterfeit Securities: Discovered forged or duplicated certificates.
  • ✅ Recoveries: If a previously reported missing security is found, it must be reported as a "Recovery" on the same form.

Critical Deadlines

Theft

Report to the SEC/SIC and the FBI within 1 business day of discovery.

Loss/Missing

Report within 1 business day if criminal activity is suspected; otherwise within 2 business days after internal search.

Counterfeit

Report to the SEC/SIC and the FBI within 2 business days of discovery.

Form Details

Key Information Fields

Field 1

Reporting Institution

Details of the firm filing the report, including their FIN/SIC Identifier Number.

Field 2

Type of Report

Selection between "Loss," "Recovery," or "Update" to an existing filing.

Field 5-13

Security Identification

Specifics of the security: Issuer name, CUSIP number, Certificate/Serial numbers, and Denomination (shares or par value).

Field 15

Criminal Suspicion

Indication if the loss is believed to be the result of a criminal act (triggers FBI notification).

Signatures

The form must be signed by a natural person authorized by the reporting institution. This individual attests to the accuracy of the report and acknowledges that providing false information is a felony under federal law.
