Institutional Holdings (13F)

13F-HR

Quarterly Institutional Portfolio Holdings

Frequency/When Within 45 days after each calendar quarter-end (February 14, May 15, August 14, November 14).
Who Files Investment managers exercising investment discretion over $100M+ in US equity securities.
Authority SEC Mandate

Page Contents

General Information

General Information & Instructions

Filing Summary

  • What: Discloses the complete US equity portfolio of institutional investment managers with $100M+ in US equities. Shows every position: issuer name, CUSIP, share class, number of shares, market value, and investment discretion. Filed quarterly.
  • Why: The most analyzed quarterly filing in equity markets. Powers 'copycat investing' — tracking Berkshire, Bridgewater, Tiger Global, and thousands of hedge funds. Institutional flow data drives Yahoo Finance Holders tab, Finviz, Wisesheets, TipRanks Smart Portfolio. 13F analysis is an entire investment industry.
  • When: Within 45 days after each calendar quarter-end (February 14, May 15, August 14, November 14).
  • Who: Investment managers exercising investment discretion over $100M+ in US equity securities.

Professional Insight

13Fs allow you to see what 'The Smart Money' is doing. You can track which sectors managers are rotating into or witness the exact moment Warren Buffett starts selling a position. Many retail strategies involve 'Copycat Investing,' which replicates the top holdings of elite hedge funds based on their 13F disclosures.

Understanding the 13F-HR Filing

A quarterly window into the portfolios of the world's largest investment managers (those with $100M+ in US equities). It lists all long positions, including share counts and market values. High-frequency 13Fs (like those from Renaissance Technologies) or value-focused 13Fs (like Berkshire Hathaway) are dissected by the entire financial community.

ENRICHED DETAILS

Key Form Components

Deep-dive details regarding the specific regulatory requirements and analytical focuses for this filing:

Holdings

Institutional Table

The primary data table listing every US-listed security held by the fund manager, including share count and market value.

Value

Market Value

Reports the total value of the positions in thousands of dollars, used to calculate the 'AUM' (Assets Under Management) of the firm.

Change

Sequential Analysis

By comparing with the prior 13F-HR, analysts identify 'New Positions' and 'Exited Positions,' or where a manager is significantly increasing their stake.
