General Information & Instructions

Filing Summary

  • What: Foreign equivalent of 10-K. Audited annual financials under IFRS (or home-country GAAP with US GAAP reconciliation), MD&A, business description, and risk factors. Used by all ADR companies.
  • Why: Only audited source for ADR stock financials. Needed to fairly compare ASML, Alibaba, Toyota, Samsung ADRs against US peers. Sites like Stockanalysis.com pull ADR data directly from 20-F filings.
  • When: Within 120 days (4 months) after fiscal year-end.
  • Who: Foreign private issuers (FPIs) listed on US exchanges.

Professional Insight

The only audited source for ADR stock financials. It allows investors to compare foreign giants against US domestic peers by normalizing financial reporting. Analysts look for unique international risk factors (currency, geopolitical, foreign tax) and 'Material Weaknesses' in internal controls that are more common in non-US firms.

Understanding the 20-F Filing

The foreign private issuer's equivalent of a 10-K. It is the primary annual disclosure for non-US companies listed on US exchanges (ADRs). It features audited financials, often under IFRS, and includes a critical mandatory reconciliation to US GAAP for certain items. It also covers business risks, governance, and director backgrounds for international entities like ASML, Alibaba, or Sony.

Filing Requirements and Compliance

Ensuring compliance with SEC regulations requires timely and accurate submission of the 20-F. The mandated timeframe for this form is Within 120 days (4 months) after fiscal year-end. . Filing entities—specifically Foreign private issuers (FPIs) listed on US exchanges. —must adhere rigidly to these deadlines to avoid regulatory penalties, maintain good standing on public exchanges, and preserve investor trust.

Submitting the document typically involves formatting the data via EDGAR (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval system), often utilizing standardized XBRL tagging to allow for immediate programmatic analysis by market participants. Delays or material omissions in these disclosures can result in severe repercussions, making a streamlined reporting process essential for modern corporate compliance.

STRUCTURE

Key Form Components

This SEC filing consists of mandatory disclosures tailored to its specific regulatory purpose. Typical components include:

Item 3.D

Risk Factors

Critical disclosure of risks specific to the foreign issuer, including currency volatility, local political stability, and international regulatory shifts.

Item 5

Operating Review

The foreign equivalent of MD&A. Management explains financial results under IFRS or local GAAP and provides a reconciliation to US GAAP metrics.

Item 8

Financial Info

Audited semi-annual or annual financials. This section is the primary source for evaluating the creditworthiness and profitability of international ADR stocks.

Item 16

Governance

Discloses any significant differences between the company's home-country governance practices and the NYSE/Nasdaq standards for US firms.

APPLICATION

Regulatory Context

This form serves a distinct function within the SEC's EDGAR database, maintaining market transparency and ensuring fair access to crucial institutional data.

Integrity

Market Integrity

By mandating standardized disclosures, the SEC ensures all market participants have equal access to material information.

Compliance

Legal Compliance

Filers must adhere to strict formatting and timeline rules. Failure to submit or materially misrepresenting facts results in significant enforcement actions.

Signatures

The report must be signed by the registrant and by its authorized officers/directors in accordance with SEC rules.
