10-K Annual Report Annual Reports Comprehensive annual report: audited financials (income statement, balance sheet, cash flows), MD&A, business description, risk factors, properties, legal proceedings, executive compensation, and internal controls assessment. All domestic public companies registered under Exchange Act Sections 12 or 15(d). 60 days (large accelerated), 75 days (accelerated), or 90 days (non-accelerated) after fiscal year-end. Gold standard of financial disclosure. Every revenue, EPS, margin, and debt metric on Yahoo Finance / Finviz / Macrotrends flows from 10-K data. Without it you cannot build a proper DCF, compare historical financials, or verify audited results. 10-KT Annual Report – Transition Period Annual Reports Annual report for a stub period when a company changes its fiscal year-end. Covers the transition months between old and new fiscal year. Domestic public companies changing their fiscal year-end. Same timeframe as regular 10-K, measured from transition period end. Catches financial data for irregular periods that would otherwise create gaps in your multi-year financial analysis. 20-F Annual Report – Foreign Private Issuers Annual Reports Foreign equivalent of 10-K. Audited annual financials under IFRS (or home-country GAAP with US GAAP reconciliation), MD&A, business description, and risk factors. Used by all ADR companies. Foreign private issuers (FPIs) listed on US exchanges. Within 120 days (4 months) after fiscal year-end. Only audited source for ADR stock financials. Needed to fairly compare ASML, Alibaba, Toyota, Samsung ADRs against US peers. Sites like Stockanalysis.com pull ADR data directly from 20-F filings. 40-F Annual Report – Canadian Companies (MJDS) Annual Reports Annual report for Canadian companies using the Multijurisdictional Disclosure System. Allows use of Canadian disclosure documents accepted by SEC. Qualifying Canadian companies listed on US exchanges under MJDS. Same day as the issuer's annual report is due to be filed with Canadian securities regulators. Source of annual financials for major Canadian companies trading on US exchanges (e.g., Shopify, TD Bank, Canadian Natural Resources). 18-K Annual Report – Foreign Governments Annual Reports Annual report filed by foreign governments and their agencies that issue securities in the US (e.g., sovereign bonds). Includes macroeconomic data, debt levels, and government finances. Foreign governments, central banks, and government agencies that have issued securities in US markets. Typically within 9 months after fiscal year-end. Used by sovereign bond investors and EM (emerging market) debt analysts to assess country credit risk, GDP, debt-to-GDP ratios, and fiscal position. 11-K Annual Report – Employee Stock Plans Annual Reports Annual report specifically for employee stock purchase plans, 401(k) plans, or savings plans that invest in company securities. Reveals actual shares held by employees in company stock, a proxy for internal confidence in the stock. 11-KT Annual Report – Employee Plans (Transition) Annual Reports Transition period version of Form 11-K when the plan changes its fiscal year. Ensures continuity of employee stock plan data during fiscal year changes. 10-Q Quarterly Report Quarterly Reports Unaudited quarterly financial statements for Q1, Q2, Q3.
