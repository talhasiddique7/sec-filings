10-K Annual Report Annual Reports
Comprehensive annual report: audited financials (income statement, balance sheet, cash flows), MD&A, business description, risk factors, properties, legal proceedings, executive compensation, and internal controls assessment.
All domestic public companies registered under Exchange Act Sections 12 or 15(d).
60 days (large accelerated), 75 days (accelerated), or 90 days (non-accelerated) after fiscal year-end.
Gold standard of financial disclosure. Every revenue, EPS, margin, and debt metric on Yahoo Finance / Finviz / Macrotrends flows from 10-K data. Without it you cannot build a proper DCF, compare historical financials, or verify audited results.
20-F Annual Report ΓÇô Foreign Private Issuers Annual Reports
Foreign equivalent of 10-K. Audited annual financials under IFRS (or home-country GAAP with US GAAP reconciliation), MD&A, business description, and risk factors. Used by all ADR companies.
Foreign private issuers (FPIs) listed on US exchanges. Within 120 days (4 months) after fiscal year-end.
Only audited source for ADR stock financials. Needed to fairly compare ASML, Alibaba, Toyota, Samsung ADRs against US peers. Sites like Stockanalysis.com pull ADR data directly from 20-F filings.
40-F Annual Report ΓÇô Canadian Companies (MJDS) Annual Reports
Annual report for Canadian companies using the Multijurisdictional Disclosure System. Allows use of Canadian disclosure documents accepted by SEC.
Qualifying Canadian companies listed on US exchanges under MJDS.
Same day as the issuer's annual report is due to be filed with Canadian securities regulators.
Source of annual financials for major Canadian companies trading on US exchanges (e.g., Shopify, TD Bank, Canadian Natural Resources).
18-K Annual Report ΓÇô Foreign Governments Annual Reports
Annual report filed by foreign governments and their agencies that issue securities in the US (e.g., sovereign bonds). Includes macroeconomic data, debt levels, and government finances.
Foreign governments, central banks, and government agencies that have issued securities in US markets.
Typically within 9 months after fiscal year-end.
Used by sovereign bond investors and EM (emerging market) debt analysts to assess country credit risk, GDP, debt-to-GDP ratios, and fiscal position.
11-K Annual Report ΓÇô Employee Stock Plans Annual Reports
Annual report specifically for employee stock purchase plans, 401(k) plans, or savings plans that invest in company securities. Contains audited plan financials.
Companies whose employee benefit/savings plans hold company securities registered under the Securities Act.
Within 90 days after plan's fiscal year-end (180 days for plans subject to ERISA).
Used in equity compensation analysis. Reveals actual shares held by employees in company stock, a proxy for internal confidence in the stock.
10-Q Quarterly Report Quarterly Reports
Unaudited quarterly financial statements for Q1, Q2, Q3. Includes balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement, MD&A, and updates on material developments. Q4 is covered by 10-K.
All domestic Exchange Act reporting companies.
40 days (large accelerated/accelerated) or 45 days (non-accelerated) after each of first three fiscal quarter-ends.
Powers all quarterly financial tracking ΓÇö revenue growth, EPS beat/miss, margin trends, cash burn. Every earnings tracker and stock screener (Yahoo Finance, Finviz, Macrotrends) sources quarterly data from 10-Q. Most frequently used periodic filing for active investors.
8-K Current Report ΓÇô Material Events Current / Event Reports
Reports material corporate events across 33 categories: earnings releases (Exhibit 99.1), M&A announcements, CEO/CFO changes, bankruptcy filings, dividend declarations, stock buyback programs, credit agreement amendments, debt offerings, SEC investigation notices, and more.
All domestic Exchange Act reporting companies. Within 4 business days of the triggering event.
The most market-moving filing. Earnings announcements, M&A deals, management changes ΓÇö all hit EDGAR as 8-Ks first. Every news feed on Yahoo Finance, MarketBeat, Seeking Alpha, and TipRanks parses 8-K filings in real time. Missing 8-K monitoring means hearing news hours or days late.
6-K Foreign Issuer Current Report Current / Event Reports
Foreign private issuers' equivalent of the 8-K. Reports material information that the FPI disclosed or was required to disclose in its home country or on any foreign stock exchange.
Foreign private issuers registered under Section 12 of the Exchange Act. Promptly after information is made public in home country.
The only way to track material news from ADR companies ΓÇö earnings results from ASML, Shell, Alibaba, Sony all arrive via 6-K. Any platform covering international stocks monitors 6-K filings.
1-U Current Report ΓÇô Regulation A Issuers Current / Event Reports
Current report for companies that went public via Regulation A offering (Tier 2). Discloses material events similar to an 8-K but for smaller Reg A companies.
Companies with ongoing Reg A Tier 2 offerings or reporting obligations. Within 4 business days of triggering event.
Tracks material news for smaller, non-NYSE/NASDAQ companies that used Reg A mini-IPO route. Niche but important for micro-cap and retail crowdfunding investors.
S-1 IPO Registration Statement Registration Statements (S-Series)
Primary registration for domestic companies going public (IPO) or registering a new class of securities. Contains: business overview, financials (3 years audited), risk factors, use of proceeds, management, and underwriter info.
Domestic companies conducting first public offering.
Filed before IPO; effective after SEC review. Must be public at least 15 days before roadshow begins.
The IPO document. Contains all historical financials and business narrative for a pre-IPO company. Every IPO-focused tool on Yahoo Finance, Seeking Alpha, and TipRanks links to the S-1 as the definitive source. Essential reading before investing in any new listing.

Allows seasoned companies to register large amounts of securities (stock, bonds, warrants) in advance and offer them over 3 years using short-form prospectus supplements. Enables rapid capital markets access.

Dilution signal. Every S-3 filing means the company can issue new shares at will. ATM (at-the-market) programs are shelf offerings. MarketBeat and Seeking Alpha flag S-3 filings as share overhang risk. Essential for monitoring dilution exposure.
